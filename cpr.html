CPR

Cardiac Emergencies


Adult and Child CPR

Adult CPR
  1. Move clothes out of the way.
  2. Position the adult face up.
  3. Compressions:
    1. Put the heel of 1 hand between the nipples on the lower half of the breast bone and clasp your other hand on top of the hand against the person's chest. Push hard and fast ("Another One Bites the Dust").
      1. Compress about 2 inches deep.
      2. Give at least 100 compressions per minute.
      3. Let the chest come back up to its normal position.
    2. Try not to interrupt compression for more than a few seconds.
  4. Breaths:
    1. After 30 compressions, open the airway with a head tilt/chin lift.
    2. Check the mouth for foreign objects and remove any objects you see.
    3. After the airway is open and clear, take a normal breath.
    4. Pinch the nose shut. Cover the person's mouth with your mouth.
    5. Give 2 breaths (blow for 1 second each). Watch for the chest to begin to rise as you give each breath.
  5. Repeat sets of 30 compressions and 2 breaths.
  6. If you are alone after 5 sets of 30 compressions and 2 breaths, phone 9-1-1, and then resume sets of 30:2.
  7. When the AED arrives:
    1. Turn it on by lifting the lid or pressing the "ON" button.
    2. Use adult pads/key or switch.
    3. Follow the prompts.
  8. Keep giving sets of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until the adult starts to breathe or move, until someone with more advanced training arrives and takes over, or you become too exhausted to continue.

Infant CPR

Infant CPR
  1. Move clothes out of the way.
  2. Position the infant face up. Make sure to turn/move them in a way that limits neck movement.
  3. Compressions:
    1. Place 2 fingers just below the nipple line. Push straight down. Give 30 compressions.
      1. Compress about 1.5 inches deep.
      2. Give at least 100 compressions per minute.
      3. Let the chest come back up to its normal position.
    2. Try not to interrupt compressions for more than a few seconds.
  4. Breaths:
    1. After 30 compressions, open the airway with a head tilt/chin lift.
    2. Check the mouth for foreign objects and remove any objects you see.
    3. After the airway is open and clear, take a normal breath.
    4. Cover the infant's mouth and nose with your mouth.
    5. Give 2 breaths (blow for 1 second each). Watch for the chest to begin to rise as you give each breath. (Be sure not to blow too hard)
  5. Repeat sets of 30 compressions and 2 breaths.
  6. If you are alone after 5 sets of 30 compressions and 2 breaths, phone 9-1-1, and then resume sets of 30:2.
  7. Keep giving sets of 30 compression and 2 breaths until the infant starts to breathe or move, until someone with more advanced training arrives and takes over, or you become too exhausted to continue.
